    4. make somebody enthusiastic

  4. [transitive] to make somebody feel very excited about something or interested in something
    • fire somebody with something The talk had fired her with enthusiasm for the project.
    • fire something The stories fired his imagination
    Extra Examples
    • The speaker knew how to fire the blood.
    • His imagination had been fired by the film.
    Topics Feelingsc2

    5. of engine

  5. [intransitive] when an engine fires, an electrical spark is produced that makes the fuel burn and the engine start to work

    6. -fired

  6. (in adjectives) using the fuel mentioned in order to operate
    • gas-fired central heating
    7.

    clay objects

  7. [transitive] fire something to heat a clay object to make it hard and strong
    • to fire pottery
    • to fire bricks in a kiln
    Topics Artc2
    8. Word OriginOld English fȳr (noun), fȳrian ‘supply with material for a fire’, of West Germanic origin; related to Dutch vuur and German Feuer.
Idioms
fire questions, insults, etc. at somebody
  1. to ask somebody a lot of questions one after another or make a lot of comments very quickly
    • The room was full of journalists, all firing questions at them.
working/firing on all cylinders
  1. (informal) using all your energy to do something; working as well as possible
